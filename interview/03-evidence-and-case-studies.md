# Evidence And Case Studies

Populates: `kb/projects/`, `kb/experience/`, `kb/skills/`, `kb/contacts/`, `kb/positioning-rules/disclosure.md`, and `kb/GAPS.md`.

You are forcing evidence into shape. Do not let me hide behind adjectives. Every signature story needs a number, an adoption answer, and attribution boundaries.

## T1 mock essay

Ask:

> Describe the most complex thing you have shipped, fixed, or pulled off. What was hard, what changed, and what is the number that makes this land?

Then ask:

> Was it actually adopted, used, paid for, cited, reused, or approved? Or was it just produced?

For each candidate case study, mine:

- Before state.
- Action taken.
- After state.
- Numbers: time, money, users, volume, frequency, accuracy, adoption.
- Who used it.
- Who else contributed.
- What part was mine.
- What proof exists: public URL, artifact, testimonial, screenshot, repo, talk, post.

## T2 strawman artifact

Draft a deliberately incomplete case-study paragraph from what is known. Make it a little wrong. Then ask me:

> Read this aloud. Mark anything wrong, missing, overstated, understated, or cringe.

Ask:

> What is the sentence you disagree with most, and what is the truer version?

## Ratings and evidence inventory

Ask for benchmark-anchored self-ratings:

- What external benchmark proves the level: cert, test, shipped artifact, user count, reference, public evidence?
- Where am I conservative because I lack formal credentials?
- Where would overclaiming damage trust?

Build a public-evidence inventory:

- GitHub.
- LinkedIn.
- Blog or essays.
- Talks or workshops.
- Public artifacts.
- Things that exist but should stay private.

Append open questions to `kb/GAPS.md` with date, case-study name, missing metric, and whether adoption is confirmed.

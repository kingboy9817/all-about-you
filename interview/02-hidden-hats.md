# Hidden Hats

Populates: `kb/experience/`, `kb/skills/`, `kb/projects/`, `kb/contacts/`, and `kb/GAPS.md`.

You are looking for unlabeled work. Do not ask "what other hats did you wear?" That makes me recall labels I may not have. Use recognition.

Ask each probe and make me answer yes, no, or "maybe, pull that thread":

- Have you ever been the person new joiners got sent to, even though onboarding was not your job? (training / enablement)
- Ever calmed down angry users, customers, or community members during an outage, scandal, or panic? (crisis communications)
- Ever written the doc, guide, or template everyone actually uses? (technical writing)
- Ever been pulled into a sales call or demo because you could explain the thing? (sales engineering / solutions)
- Ever screened candidates, sat in interviews, or trained your own replacement? (hiring / management)
- Ever run the Discord, Slack, forum, or other community space: moderation, recruiting mods, setting rules? (community and platform ops)
- Ever been the unofficial spokesperson, the one who wrote the announcement or faced users when something changed? (PR / comms)
- Ever translated between languages, cultures, technical and non-technical people, or user and builder? (localization / developer relations)
- Ever kept an important relationship warm for the organization: partner, investor, major customer, referrer? (partnerships / business development)
- Ever organized the offsite, meetup, launch event, or internal ritual? (events / programs)
- Ever built a spreadsheet, script, checklist, or workflow the team came to depend on? (internal tooling / automation)
- Ever quietly checked others' work before it went out? (QA / editorial review)

For every hit, ask:

> Regardless of titles, who did people actually come to for this, and was that you?

Then run the canonical-label workshop:

> The raw story is what happened. The label is how the market finds it. Is this developer relations, solutions engineering, program management, community ops, enablement, QA, comms, or something else? Which label feels accurate, and which label feels fake?

## Confirm-back

Return a shadow org chart: function, who came to whom, evidence, market label, confidence.

## Write-back

Create or update relevant files in `kb/experience/`, `kb/skills/`, `kb/projects/`, and `kb/contacts/`. Store both raw language and canonical labels.

Append open questions to `kb/GAPS.md` with date, hidden-hat label, and the evidence still missing.

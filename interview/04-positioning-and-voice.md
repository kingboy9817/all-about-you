Populates: `kb/positioning-rules/voice.md`, `kb/positioning-rules/claims-policy.md`, `kb/positioning-rules/presentation.md`, `kb/positioning-rules/disclosure.md`, and `kb/GAPS.md`.

You are extracting rules the drafting engine must obey. Do not ask me for "tone." Give me options to reject.

## Ethos

Ask:

> When someone reads your materials cold, what should they understand first? What should they never see unless directly relevant?

Mine:

- Lead-with-strength rules.
- Topics never to raise unprompted.
- Weaknesses that should not be pre-defended.
- Reputation-sensitive facts and public-safe framings.

## Claims policy

Ask:

> Where is your line between strong framing and lying? Give examples of claims that are allowed, claims that are forbidden, and claims that need a footnote.

Capture:

- Ownership vs collaboration.
- Metrics: exact, rough, forbidden.
- Title inflation ceiling.
- Employer or customer anonymization boundaries.
- What must be confirmed before external use.

## T7 persona options

Draft three contrasting one-paragraph personas from the KB so far. Make them meaningfully different. Example shapes:

1. The practical operator who turns messy work into repeatable systems.
2. The translator between technical builders and non-technical users.
3. The evidence-driven generalist who can sell, teach, and ship.

Ask me:

> Which is most wrong? Which parts are true? What would the hybrid say if it were honest and not trying to impress everyone?

Then ask:

> Which words sound like me, even if a generic tone filter would delete them? Which words should never appear?

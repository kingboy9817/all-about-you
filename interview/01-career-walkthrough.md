# Career Walkthrough

Populates: `kb/experience/`, `kb/skills/`, `kb/projects/`, `kb/goals/`, and `kb/GAPS.md`.

You are interviewing me for role history. Do not ask for a resume recap. Mine the parts a resume hides: internal title vs external title, the job under the job, brand vs legal employer, why I left, and the most senior title I can honestly tolerate.

## Monologue first

Ask me:

> Walk me through every role, but do it messily. Start wherever the story has heat. For each period, tell me what the official title said, what you actually did, who depended on you, why you left, and what title would feel honest to an outsider.

Then give me this numbered mining list. I can answer in order or brain-dump:

1. What was the legal employer, public brand, and any renamed entity?
2. What was your internal title, LinkedIn title, and outsider-friendly title?
3. What was the job under the job: the separate functions people actually came to you for?
4. What changed because you were there? Give numbers: how many, how often, how much, how fast.
5. Why did you leave or stop doing that work? Do not sanitize it yet.
6. What title ceiling should drafts never exceed without your explicit approval?

## Quick factual asks

After the monologue, ask for missing facts in a fast pass:

- Start and end dates.
- Location and work pattern.
- Employment type.
- Team or manager if relevant.
- Skills actually used.
- Projects that deserve separate files.

## Confirm-back

Summarize each role as:

- Employer / brand / legal entity.
- Internal title / external title / title ceiling.
- Job-under-the-job labels.
- Why-I-left note.
- Evidence and missing numbers.

Make me correct it.

## Write-back

Create or update schema-valid files in `kb/experience/`, `kb/skills/`, `kb/projects/`, and `kb/goals/`. Mark perishable facts with `last_confirmed`, `durability: perishable`, and `ttl_days`.

Append open questions to `kb/GAPS.md` with date, role, and why the missing fact matters.

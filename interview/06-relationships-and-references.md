# Relationships And References

Populates: `kb/contacts/`, `kb/experience/`, `kb/positioning-rules/disclosure.md`, and `kb/GAPS.md`.

You are mapping relationships, not collecting names. A reference is useful only if we know what they personally saw.

## Relationship inventory

Ask:

> Who could vouch for you in their own words? For each person, tell me how you met, what they watched you do, how the relationship works, and what they would say if they were honest rather than diplomatic.

Mine:

- Name.
- Current role and company.
- How we met.
- Shared work or context.
- Cadence and channel.
- What they directly observed.
- What they should not be asked to vouch for.
- Whether they may be named unprompted.

## T4 reference-letter simulation

For each serious contact, ask:

> If they get one sentence on why you are good at this, what is the sentence, and what story proves it?

Then ask:

> If their account conflicts with your self-report, whose account wins and why?

Capture source-of-truth precedence:

- Self-report.
- Manager.
- Client.
- Public artifact.
- Written testimonial.
- Recruiter or referrer.

## Disclosure policy

Ask:

> Before this ever goes external, who can be named, who must be anonymized, and who should never appear unless I approve it in that exact context?

## Write-back

Create or update schema-valid files in `kb/contacts/` and relevant `kb/experience/` entries. Update `kb/positioning-rules/disclosure.md` with reference/referrer mention policy and anonymization rules.

Append open questions to `kb/GAPS.md` with date, contact, and what permission or evidence is missing.
